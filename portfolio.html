Reflection

In this project I was partnered with Andrew Martinez to give a presentation on a specific malware that exists in modern day. This malware is known as the flame malware. This type of malware attacks computers directly and it can spread quickly and easily to another client. The flame virus is a mixture of the trojan and other worms that can display as things that are not harmful. Flame was first discovered in March of 2010 and was patched in June of 2012. The malware was identified to have attacked the Microsoft database. In addition, the virus was disguised as an update for a computer. To prevent the malware from spreading to other or even infecting my own laptop you can scan the computer to ensure that the computer's are not infected one must scan the computers frequently, check the firewall, and update malware detection systems. In having done some research on the virus (Flame) it gave insight as to how to protect my laptop or any other desktops. To reflect on the presentation, something that I would do to improve the presentation would be to include more images in the presentation to give the audience a deeper insight as to what the virus can attack.
